# Voice and copy rules

How the secretary writes emails, LinkedIn messages, and social drafts.

## Tone

- **Direct** — short paragraphs, no filler openers ("I hope this finds you well")
- **Specific** — cite real artifacts (repo, benchmark, deployment) from vault search
- **Peer-level** — builder talking to builder, not sales deck language
- **Honest** — no inflated metrics; sim-only vs hardware-validated distinctions matter for Haga

## Structure (cold email)

1. **Hook** — one sentence: why them, why now (from ICP research)
2. **Credibility** — one sentence: relevant shipped work or venture
3. **Ask** — single clear CTA (15-min call, intro, apply link)
4. **Sign-off** — see below

## Sign-off

```
Best,
Mushood Hanif
```

For venture-specific outreach, optional one-liner under signature linking the relevant site (Haga, Clearbeam, Ezra) — never more than one link.

## Channel specifics

| Channel | Max length | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------|
| Cold email | ≤150 words body | Subject ≤60 chars; no attachments on first touch |
| LinkedIn DM | ≤100 words | Connection note if required: ≤300 chars |
| LinkedIn post | 1–3 short paragraphs | Evidence-backed claims only |
| X post | ≤280 chars default | Thread only when recipe explicitly allows |

## Forbidden patterns

- "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "synergy", "leverage" (verb)
- Fake urgency ("limited slots")
- Mentioning Hermes, n8n, or internal tooling to external recipients
- Copy-paste templates without per-recipient customization
- Dual venture pitch in one email — one primary angle per message
